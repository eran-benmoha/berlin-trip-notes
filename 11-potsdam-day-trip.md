# Day Trip: Berlin → Potsdam

> **Date**: TBD (fits any day during 18.2–22.2; best on **20.2 or 21.2** when you have a full day)
> **Duration**: ~6–8 hours (leave morning, back by evening)
> **Transit zone**: You need an **ABC ticket** (Potsdam is in zone C). If you have the **Berlin WelcomeCard ABC**, it already covers this.

---

## Getting there from Wolliner Str 12b

### Recommended route (~50–55 min door to door)

1. **Walk** to **S+U Gesundbrunnen** (~10 min) or **U Bernauer Straße** (U8, ~6 min)
2. **S1** from **Gesundbrunnen → Potsdam Hauptbahnhof** (direct, ~45 min)
   - Runs every 10–20 min
   - This is the easiest option — one train, no changes

### Alternative route (~45 min, requires a change)

1. **Tram M10** from "Wolliner Str." → **Berlin Hauptbahnhof**
2. **RE1** (Regional Express) from **Berlin Hbf → Potsdam Hbf** (~25 min)
   - Faster train but you have a transfer

### Return

Same routes in reverse. Last S1 from Potsdam Hbf back toward Gesundbrunnen is usually around **~00:30** (check on the day).

## Key sights in Potsdam

### Sanssouci Park & Palace
- **What**: Frederick the Great's summer palace (1747) — often called the "Prussian Versailles"
- **Park**: Free, open all day. Beautiful even in winter — wide paths, terraced vineyards, Orangery, New Palace at the far end
- **Palace interior**: Timed ticket, **~€14**. Book at [spsg.de](https://tickets.spsg.de) if you want to go inside (worth it but not essential — the exterior and park are the main draw)
- **Getting there from Potsdam Hbf**: **Bus 695** to "Schloss Sanssouci" (~15 min) or **Bus 605/606** to "Schloss Sanssouci" — or walk (~25 min through the park from the west entrance)

### Dutch Quarter (Holländisches Viertel)
- **What**: 134 red-brick houses built in the 1730s for Dutch craftsmen — the largest Dutch settlement outside the Netherlands
- **Vibe**: Cozy, independent shops, antiques, cafés, galleries
- **Location**: Between Nauener Tor and Brandenburger Straße, ~15 min walk from Potsdam Hbf

### Brandenburger Straße
- **What**: Potsdam's main pedestrian shopping street
- **Useful for**: Warm-up stops, cafés, souvenir browsing

### Alter Markt & Nikolaikirche
- **What**: Potsdam's historic old market square with the domed Church of St. Nicholas
- **Location**: Near the Havel river, ~10 min walk from Potsdam Hbf

### Cecilienhof Palace (only if you have extra time)
- **What**: Where the 1945 Potsdam Conference took place (Truman, Stalin, Churchill/Attlee)
- **Ticket**: ~€10
- **Getting there**: Bus 603 from the city center, or a pleasant walk through the New Garden

## Practical tips

- **February weather**: Expect **0–5 °C**, possibly gray or drizzly. Dress in warm layers + waterproof jacket. Sanssouci Park paths can be muddy — sturdy shoes recommended.
- **Sanssouci Palace interior**: In winter (Nov–Mar), the palace is open **only Tue–Sun** and often only with **guided tours at set times**. Check [spsg.de](https://www.spsg.de/en/) the day before. The park itself is always open.
- **Cash**: Some smaller Potsdam shops and cafés are cash-only. Bring some euros.
- **Duration**: Potsdam is compact. You can see the highlights in a half-day, but a full day lets you enjoy it without rushing.
- **WelcomeCard discount**: If you have the Berlin WelcomeCard, it gives up to **50% off** at Sanssouci Palace and other SPSG palaces — show it at the ticket counter.

## Summary / quick decision helper

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| **Which day?** | Any full day works. **21.2 (Sat)** keeps it flexible — or **20.2 (Fri)** if you want Sat free for Berlin. |
| **How long?** | 6 hours (highlights only) to 8 hours (with Wannsee). |
| **Need to pre-book?** | Only if you want to go **inside** Sanssouci Palace. Everything else is walk-up. |
| **Transit cost?** | Covered by WelcomeCard ABC. Otherwise a single ABC day ticket (~€9.90). |
| **Best for a cold/rainy day?** | Partly — museums + cafés are great, but Sanssouci Park is the highlight and that's outdoors. Pick a day with decent weather if possible. |
